Miami University Alumni Facts

Being a Redhawk does not end when you walk across the stage to receive your diploma. Miami University is known for its welcoming community, and friends that turn into forever family. Graduating is a big accomplishment to most, but some say becoming a Redhawk is too. Alumni often find themselves gravitating towards each other in the outside world, eventually making the small world connection. This university is known for its strong values and love for student education. We stand and say, “Love and Honor”.

Fun facts about Miami

  • Average high school GPA is 3.9.
  • Miami University is the tenth-oldest public university in the United States.
  • "Miami Mergers" refers to Miami University alumni who marry other Miami alumni (16%). Miami Merger receive valentines cards as well on anniversaries.
  • It is believed that if one kisses their significant other or “true love” under the Upham Arch, their love will never falter, much like the love Miami has for its students.
  • It is Miami's tradition to avoid stepping on the seal out of respect for the university's history and values. If you do step on the seal, it is commonly believed that you will fail your next exam.

What does it mean to be a student at Miami University

During the 4 years of being at MU, you not only received your degree but there's much more to it. The day you receive your acceptance letter and you commit to this opportunity, you enter into a forever family. Students are learning all kinds of skills every day; communication, professional development, leadership, etc. Even though Oxford is a small college town, student life holds great significance to the university. The campus is a safe and secure environment, controlled by Oxford PD & Miami University Police. No matter your major, gender, race, religion, etc you are welcomed and loved by a Redhawk.
